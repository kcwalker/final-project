The American Revolution (more a double revolution, one to esablish independence, and later to establish a viable government)is the first major watershed moment in what would become the United States. This class will examine the causes, major players, and effects that went with this momentous anti-colonial movement, and how it would inspire others like it, such as in France, Haiti (both of which we will discuss to a degree), and Spanish America shortly thereafter.

Course Goals:

Students enrolled in the class should be able to:

  1. Identify the key players in this tumultuous period
  2. Be able to examine the roles European geopolitics and Enlightenmnt principles played
  3. Examine the roles women, blacks (both free and unfree), and lower class people played in securing American freedom (and their own, if possible)
  4. Discuss the efforts to secure the blessings of liberty (and for whom and why) during the Constitutiinal Convention
